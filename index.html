The text of the treatise. (Workbook p.4 — Arabic fully vocalized; transliteration & English are study aids.)

بِسْمِ اللهِ الرَّحْمٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ

Bismi-llāhi-r-Raḥmāni-r-Raḥīm

In the name of Allāh, the Most Merciful, the Bestower of mercy.

اِعْلَمْ رَحِمَكَ اللهُ أَنَّهُ يَجِبُ عَلَيْنَا تَعَلُّمُ أَرْبَعِ مَسَائِلَ:

Iʿlam — raḥimaka-llāh — annahu yajibu ʿalaynā taʿallumu arbaʿi masāʾil:

Know — may Allāh have mercy upon you — that it is obligatory upon us to learn four matters:

المَسَائِلُ الأَرْبَعُ — The Four Matters
#المَسْأَلَةTransliterationMeaning

💡 What is this book?
This treatise — al-Uṣūl ath-Thalāthah ("The Three Fundamental Principles") — is a short classic by Muḥammad ibn ʿAbd al-Wahhāb. It opens not with the three principles themselves, but with a foundation: four matters every Muslim must learn, act on, call to, and be patient upon. The three principles come at the end of this introduction.
🌱 Word root
العِلْم · al-ʿilm
From the root ع-ل-م (to know). The same root gives ʿālim (a scholar), maʿlūm (something known), and taʿallum (learning). Notice the book lists knowledge first — because right action must be built on knowing, not guessing.

أَجِبْ — Answer from the text:
١

How many matters is it obligatory upon us to learn?

أَرْبَعُ مَسَائِلَFour matters.
٢

What three things does "knowledge" (al-ʿilm) consist of?

مَعْرِفَةُ اللهِ، وَمَعْرِفَةُ نَبِيِّهِ، وَمَعْرِفَةُ دِينِ الإِسْلَامِ بِالأَدِلَّةِKnowing Allāh, knowing His Prophet, and knowing the religion of Islām with the proofs.
٣

After knowledge, what are the remaining three matters in order?

العَمَلُ بِهِ، ثُمَّ الدَّعْوَةُ إِلَيْهِ، ثُمَّ الصَّبْرُ عَلَى الأَذَى فِيهِActing upon it, then calling to it, then patience upon the harm.

Unit 1 · Lesson 2 — The Proof: Sūrat al-ʿAṣr
الدَّلِيلُ مِنَ القُرْآنِ — The Qurʾānic Proof

Workbook p.5 — the proof that the four matters are obligatory. Qurʾānic verses appear in braces ﴿ ﴾.

وَالدَّلِيلُ قَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَالعَصْرِ • إِنَّ الإِنْسَانَ لَفِي خُسْرٍ • إِلَّا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ وَتَوَاصَوْا بِالحَقِّ وَتَوَاصَوْا بِالصَّبْرِ﴾

Wa-d-dalīlu qawluhu taʿālā: ﴿Wa-l-ʿaṣr • inna-l-insāna la-fī khusr • illā-lladhīna āmanū wa ʿamilū-ṣ-ṣāliḥāti wa tawāṣaw bi-l-ḥaqqi wa tawāṣaw bi-ṣ-ṣabr﴾

The proof is His saying, the Most High: {By time. Indeed mankind is in loss; except those who truly believe, perform righteous deeds, encourage one another upon the truth, and encourage one another with patience.} [103:1–3]

أَقْوَالُ العُلَمَاءِ — Sayings of the Scholars

قَالَ الشَّافِعِيُّ رَحِمَهُ اللهُ: «لَوْ مَا أَنْزَلَ اللهُ حُجَّةً عَلَى خَلْقِهِ إِلَّا هَذِهِ السُّورَةَ لَكَفَتْهُمْ».

Qāla-sh-Shāfiʿiyyu raḥimahu-llāh: «Law mā anzala-llāhu ḥujjatan ʿalā khalqihi illā hādhihi-s-sūrata la-kafat-hum».

Ash-Shāfiʿī (may Allāh have mercy upon him) said: "If Allāh had not sent down to His creation any proof besides this Sūrah, it would have sufficed them."

وَقَالَ البُخَارِيُّ رَحِمَهُ اللهُ: «بَابُ العِلْمِ قَبْلَ القَوْلِ وَالعَمَلِ».

Wa qāla-l-Bukhāriyyu raḥimahu-llāh: «Bābu-l-ʿilmi qabla-l-qawli wa-l-ʿamal».

Al-Bukhārī (may Allāh have mercy upon him) said: "Chapter: Knowledge comes before speech and action."

لِقَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿فَاعْلَمْ أَنَّهُ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللهُ وَاسْتَغْفِرْ لِذَنْبِكَ﴾ فَبَدَأَ بِالعِلْمِ.

Li-qawlihi taʿālā: ﴿Fa-ʿlam annahu lā ilāha illa-llāhu wa-staghfir li-dhanbik﴾ — fa-badaʾa bi-l-ʿilm.

Because of His saying, the Most High: {So know that none has the right to be worshipped except Allāh, and ask forgiveness for your sin} [47:19] — so he began with knowledge.

📖 The workbook gives this last proof (47:19) in English only; the Qurʾānic Arabic is supplied here to complete the citation.

⭐ Why al-ʿAṣr "suffices"
Imām ash-Shāfiʿī's point is that this tiny sūrah maps the whole road to salvation in four steps: faith → righteous deeds → enjoining truth → enjoining patience. Those four mirror the four matters of Lesson 1 (knowledge & faith, action, daʿwah, patience). The book opens here on purpose.
🌱 Word root
خُسْر · khusr
From خ-س-ر (to lose). The verse says mankind is la-fī khusr — "in utter loss" — except those with the four qualities. The same root gives khasāra (a loss/deficit), the opposite of ribḥ (profit).

المَسَائِلُ الثَّلَاثُ — الأُولَى

Unit 1 · Lesson 3 — The Three Matters (1): Created with Purpose
المَتْنُ — اُنْظُرْ وَاقْرَأْ وَتَدَبَّرْ

Workbook p.6 — the three matters obligatory upon every Muslim, male and female.

اِعْلَمْ رَحِمَكَ اللهُ أَنَّهُ يَجِبُ عَلَى كُلِّ مُسْلِمٍ وَمُسْلِمَةٍ تَعَلُّمُ هَذِهِ الثَّلَاثِ مَسَائِلَ وَالعَمَلُ بِهِنَّ:

Iʿlam — raḥimaka-llāh — annahu yajibu ʿalā kulli muslimin wa muslimatin taʿallumu hādhihi-th-thalāthi masāʾila wa-l-ʿamalu bi-hinn:

Know — may Allāh have mercy upon you — that it is obligatory upon every Muslim, male and female, to learn these three matters and to act upon them:

الأُولَى: أَنَّ اللهَ خَلَقَنَا وَرَزَقَنَا وَلَمْ يَتْرُكْنَا هَمَلًا، بَلْ أَرْسَلَ إِلَيْنَا رَسُولًا، فَمَنْ أَطَاعَهُ دَخَلَ الجَنَّةَ، وَمَنْ عَصَاهُ دَخَلَ النَّارَ.

Al-ūlā: anna-llāha khalaqanā wa razaqanā wa lam yatruknā hamalan, bal arsala ilaynā rasūlan, fa-man aṭāʿahu dakhala-l-jannah, wa man ʿaṣāhu dakhala-n-nār.

First: that Allāh created us and provided for us, and did not leave us without purpose; rather He sent to us a Messenger — whoever obeys him enters Paradise, and whoever disobeys him enters the Fire.

الدَّلِيلُ — The Proof

وَالدَّلِيلُ قَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿إِنَّا أَرْسَلْنَا إِلَيْكُمْ رَسُولًا شَاهِدًا عَلَيْكُمْ كَمَا أَرْسَلْنَا إِلَى فِرْعَوْنَ رَسُولًا • فَعَصَى فِرْعَوْنُ الرَّسُولَ فَأَخَذْنَاهُ أَخْذًا وَبِيلًا﴾

Wa-d-dalīlu qawluhu taʿālā: ﴿Innā arsalnā ilaykum rasūlan shāhidan ʿalaykum kamā arsalnā ilā Firʿawna rasūlā • fa-ʿaṣā Firʿawnu-r-rasūla fa-akhadhnāhu akhdhan wabīlā﴾

The proof is His saying, the Most High: {Indeed We have sent to you a Messenger as a witness over you, just as We sent a Messenger to Pharaoh. But Pharaoh disobeyed the Messenger, so We seized him with a severe punishment.} [73:15–16]

💡 The logic of the First Matter
The argument is simple and powerful: Allāh created you and sustains you — so He would not leave you hamalan (with no purpose or guidance). Instead He sent Messengers. Therefore your response to the Messenger decides your end: Paradise or the Fire.
🌱 Word root
رَسُول · rasūl
From ر-س-ل (to send). A rasūl is "one who is sent" with a message. The same root gives risālah (a message/treatise — like this very book) and arsala ("He sent," which appears in the proof-verse).

Unit 1 · Lesson 4 — The Three Matters (2): Tawḥīd & Loyalty
المَتْنُ — اُنْظُرْ وَاقْرَأْ وَتَدَبَّرْ

Workbook p.7 — the second and third of the three matters, each with its proof.

الثَّانِيَةُ: أَنَّ اللهَ لَا يَرْضَى أَنْ يُشْرَكَ مَعَهُ أَحَدٌ فِي عِبَادَتِهِ، لَا مَلَكٌ مُقَرَّبٌ وَلَا نَبِيٌّ مُرْسَلٌ.

Ath-thāniyah: anna-llāha lā yarḍā an yushraka maʿahu aḥadun fī ʿibādatih, lā malakun muqarrabun wa lā nabiyyun mursal.

Second: that Allāh is not pleased that anyone be made a partner with Him in His worship — neither an angel brought near, nor a Prophet sent as Messenger.

وَالدَّلِيلُ قَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَأَنَّ المَسَاجِدَ لِلهِ فَلَا تَدْعُوا مَعَ اللهِ أَحَدًا﴾

Wa-d-dalīlu qawluhu taʿālā: ﴿Wa anna-l-masājida li-llāhi fa-lā tadʿū maʿa-llāhi aḥadā﴾

The proof is His saying, the Most High: {And the places of worship are for Allāh alone, so do not invoke anyone along with Allāh.} [72:18]

الثَّالِثَةُ: أَنَّ مَنْ أَطَاعَ الرَّسُولَ وَوَحَّدَ اللهَ لَا يَجُوزُ لَهُ مُوَالَاةُ مَنْ حَادَّ اللهَ وَرَسُولَهُ، وَلَوْ كَانَ أَقْرَبَ قَرِيبٍ.

Ath-thālithah: anna man aṭāʿa-r-rasūla wa waḥḥada-llāha lā yajūzu lahu muwālātu man ḥādda-llāha wa rasūlah, wa law kāna aqraba qarīb.

Third: that whoever obeys the Messenger and singles out Allāh in worship, it is not permissible for him to ally himself with those who oppose Allāh and His Messenger — even if they are his closest relatives.

الدَّلِيلُ — The Proof

وَالدَّلِيلُ قَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿لَا تَجِدُ قَوْمًا يُؤْمِنُونَ بِاللهِ وَاليَوْمِ الآخِرِ يُوَادُّونَ مَنْ حَادَّ اللهَ وَرَسُولَهُ وَلَوْ كَانُوا آبَاءَهُمْ أَوْ أَبْنَاءَهُمْ أَوْ إِخْوَانَهُمْ أَوْ عَشِيرَتَهُمْ ۚ أُولَٰئِكَ كَتَبَ فِي قُلُوبِهِمُ الإِيمَانَ وَأَيَّدَهُمْ بِرُوحٍ مِنْهُ ۖ وَيُدْخِلُهُمْ جَنَّاتٍ تَجْرِي مِنْ تَحْتِهَا الأَنْهَارُ خَالِدِينَ فِيهَا ۚ رَضِيَ اللهُ عَنْهُمْ وَرَضُوا عَنْهُ ۚ أُولَٰئِكَ حِزْبُ اللهِ ۚ أَلَا إِنَّ حِزْبَ اللهِ هُمُ المُفْلِحُونَ﴾

Wa-d-dalīlu qawluhu taʿālā: ﴿Lā tajidu qawman yuʾminūna bi-llāhi wa-l-yawmi-l-ākhiri yuwāddūna man ḥādda-llāha wa rasūlahu wa law kānū ābāʾahum aw abnāʾahum aw ikhwānahum aw ʿashīratahum… ulāʾika ḥizbu-llāh, alā inna ḥizba-llāhi humu-l-mufliḥūn﴾

The proof is His saying, the Most High: {You will not find a people who believe in Allāh and the Last Day loving those who oppose Allāh and His Messenger, even if they are their fathers, their sons, their brothers, or their kinsfolk. For such, He has inscribed faith upon their hearts and strengthened them with a spirit from Him; and He will admit them into gardens beneath which rivers flow, abiding therein forever. Allāh is pleased with them, and they with Him. They are the party of Allāh. Indeed, the party of Allāh — they are the successful.} [58:22]

📖 The workbook prints this reference as [52:18]; the verse is Sūrat al-Mujādilah 58:22.

الثَّانِيَةُAth-thāniyah — Second
  • Allāh is not pleased that any partner be made with Him in worship.
  • Not even an angel brought near or a Prophet sent.
  • Proof: {72:18} — the places of worship are for Allāh alone.
الثَّالِثَةُAth-thālithah — Third
  • One who obeys the Messenger and singles out Allāh may not ally with Allāh's enemies.
  • True even toward closest relatives.
  • Proof: {58:22} — they are the party of Allāh, the successful.
🌱 Word root
مُوَالَاة · muwālāh
From و-ل-ي (to be near, to ally). A walī is a close ally or protector; muwālāh is giving that loyalty and love. The opposite is barāʾah (disassociation) — together they form al-walāʾ wa-l-barāʾ, a core theme of this passage.
⚠️ A point often misread
The Third Matter forbids alliance and loyalty against Allāh and His Messenger — it is about religious allegiance, not about cutting off kindness to relatives. The Qurʾān elsewhere commands good treatment of parents even if they are not believers. The verse here addresses loving and siding with the enemies of the faith.

الحَنِيفِيَّةُ مِلَّةُ إِبْرَاهِيمَ

Unit 1 · Lesson 5 — Ḥanīfiyyah: the Religion of Ibrāhīm
المَتْنُ — اُنْظُرْ وَاقْرَأْ وَتَدَبَّرْ

Workbook p.8 — the pure, upright way of Ibrāhīm: worshipping Allāh alone. The greatest command and the greatest prohibition.

اِعْلَمْ أَرْشَدَكَ اللهُ لِطَاعَتِهِ أَنَّ الحَنِيفِيَّةَ مِلَّةَ إِبْرَاهِيمَ: أَنْ تَعْبُدَ اللهَ وَحْدَهُ مُخْلِصًا لَهُ الدِّينَ، وَبِذَلِكَ أَمَرَ اللهُ جَمِيعَ النَّاسِ وَخَلَقَهُمْ لَهَا.

Iʿlam — arshadaka-llāhu li-ṭāʿatih — anna-l-ḥanīfiyyata millata Ibrāhīm: an taʿbuda-llāha waḥdahu mukhliṣan lahu-d-dīn; wa bi-dhālika amara-llāhu jamīʿa-n-nāsi wa khalaqahum lahā.

Know — may Allāh guide you to His obedience — that Ḥanīfiyyah, the religion of Ibrāhīm, is that you worship Allāh alone, devoting the religion purely and sincerely to Him. With this Allāh commanded all people, and for this He created them.

كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَمَا خَلَقْتُ الجِنَّ وَالإِنْسَ إِلَّا لِيَعْبُدُونِ﴾ وَمَعْنَى «يَعْبُدُونِ»: يُوَحِّدُونِ.

Kamā qāla taʿālā: ﴿Wa mā khalaqtu-l-jinna wa-l-insa illā li-yaʿbudūn﴾ — wa maʿnā «yaʿbudūn»: yuwaḥḥidūn.

As the Most High said: {And I did not create jinn and mankind except that they should worship Me.} [51:56] The meaning of "worship Me" is: "single Me out in Tawḥīd."

وَأَعْظَمُ مَا أَمَرَ اللهُ بِهِ التَّوْحِيدُ، وَهُوَ: إِفْرَادُ اللهِ بِالعِبَادَةِ. وَأَعْظَمُ مَا نَهَى عَنْهُ الشِّرْكُ، وَهُوَ: دَعْوَةُ غَيْرِهِ مَعَهُ.

Wa aʿẓamu mā amara-llāhu bihi-t-tawḥīd, wa huwa: ifrādu-llāhi bi-l-ʿibādah. Wa aʿẓamu mā nahā ʿanhu-sh-shirk, wa huwa: daʿwatu ghayrihi maʿah.

The greatest thing Allāh commanded is Tawḥīd — singling out Allāh in worship. The greatest thing He forbade is Shirk — invoking another alongside Him.

الدَّلِيلُ — The Proof

وَالدَّلِيلُ قَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَاعْبُدُوا اللهَ وَلَا تُشْرِكُوا بِهِ شَيْئًا﴾

Wa-d-dalīlu qawluhu taʿālā: ﴿Wa-ʿbudū-llāha wa lā tushrikū bihi shayʾā﴾

The proof is His saying, the Most High: {Worship Allāh alone, and do not associate anything in worship along with Him.} [4:36]

المُصْطَلَحَانِ المِحْوَرِيَّانِ — The Two Central Terms
التَّوْحِيد · Tawḥīd
إِفْرَادُ اللهِ بِالعِبَادَةِ
Ifrādu-llāhi bi-l-ʿibādah
Singling out Allāh alone for all worship. The greatest thing Allāh commanded — the very purpose for which jinn and mankind were created.
الشِّرْك · Shirk
دَعْوَةُ غَيْرِهِ مَعَهُ
Daʿwatu ghayrihi maʿah
Invoking another alongside Him. The greatest thing Allāh forbade — the opposite and undoing of Tawḥīd.
🌱 Word root
التَّوْحِيد · at-tawḥīd
From و-ح-د (to be one). Tawḥīd literally means "making one / declaring one." The same root gives wāḥid (one), aḥad (the Unique One), and the verb yuwaḥḥidūn ("they single Him out") that the book uses to explain the meaning of "worship Me."
⭐ The hinge of the whole religion
The author distills everything to one contrast: the greatest command is Tawḥīd and the greatest prohibition is Shirk. Hold these two together and the rest of the treatise falls into place — the three principles that follow are simply Tawḥīd applied to your Lord, your religion, and your Prophet.

The First Principle — Knowing Your Lord

الأَصْلُ الأَوَّلُ · الدَّرْسُ «٧»

مَنْ رَبُّكَ؟

Unit 2 · Lesson 7 — Who Is Your Lord?
المَتْنُ — اُنْظُرْ وَاقْرَأْ وَتَدَبَّرْ

Workbook pp.9–10 — the First Principle opens with the question every person must answer.

💡 The First Principle
Having finished the introduction, the author now takes the first of the three principles: maʿrifatu-l-ʿabdi rabbah — the servant's knowledge of his Lord. It unfolds in three questions: Who is your Lord? How did you come to know Him? What does worshipping Him mean?

فَإِذَا قِيلَ لَكَ: مَنْ رَبُّكَ؟ فَقُلْ: رَبِّيَ اللهُ الَّذِي رَبَّانِي وَرَبَّى جَمِيعَ العَالَمِينَ بِنِعَمِهِ، وَهُوَ مَعْبُودِي لَيْسَ لِي مَعْبُودٌ سِوَاهُ.

Fa-idhā qīla laka: man rabbuk? Fa-qul: rabbiya-llāhu-lladhī rabbānī wa rabbā jamīʿa-l-ʿālamīna bi-niʿamih, wa huwa maʿbūdī laysa lī maʿbūdun siwāh.

So if it is said to you: "Who is your Lord?" Then say: "My Lord is Allāh, who nurtured me and nurtured all of creation with His blessings. He is the One I worship; I have no deity besides Him."

وَالدَّلِيلُ قَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿الحَمْدُ لِلهِ رَبِّ العَالَمِينَ﴾ وَكُلُّ مَنْ سِوَى اللهِ عَالَمٌ، وَأَنَا وَاحِدٌ مِنْ ذَلِكَ العَالَمِ.

Wa-d-dalīlu qawluhu taʿālā: ﴿Al-ḥamdu li-llāhi rabbi-l-ʿālamīn﴾ — wa kullu man siwā-llāhi ʿālam, wa anā wāḥidun min dhālika-l-ʿālam.

The proof is His saying, the Most High: {All praise is for Allāh, Lord of all creation} [1:1]. Everything besides Allāh is a created being, and I am one of that creation.

🌱 Word root
الرَّبّ · ar-rabb
From ر-ب-ب — to nurture, sustain, and own. A rabb is the One who creates, owns, and gradually brings to maturity. Notice the verb rabbānī ("He nurtured me") shares this root — your Lord is the One who raised and sustains you.
بِمَ عَرَفْتَ رَبَّكَ؟ — How Did You Come to Know Him?

فَإِذَا قِيلَ لَكَ: بِمَ عَرَفْتَ رَبَّكَ؟ فَقُلْ: بِآيَاتِهِ وَمَخْلُوقَاتِهِ. وَمِنْ آيَاتِهِ: اللَّيْلُ وَالنَّهَارُ، وَالشَّمْسُ وَالقَمَرُ. وَمِنْ مَخْلُوقَاتِهِ: السَّمَاوَاتُ السَّبْعُ وَالأَرَضُونَ السَّبْعُ وَمَنْ فِيهِنَّ وَمَا بَيْنَهُمَا.

Fa-idhā qīla laka: bima ʿarafta rabbak? Fa-qul: bi-āyātihi wa makhlūqātih. Wa min āyātihi: al-laylu wa-n-nahār, wa-sh-shamsu wa-l-qamar. Wa min makhlūqātihi: as-samāwātu-s-sabʿu wa-l-araḍūna-s-sabʿu wa man fīhinna wa mā baynahumā.

So if it is said: "How did you come to know your Lord?" Then say: "Through His signs and His creations." From His signs are the night and day, the sun and the moon. From His creations are the seven heavens, the seven earths, all within them, and all between them.

وَالدَّلِيلُ قَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَمِنْ آيَاتِهِ اللَّيْلُ وَالنَّهَارُ وَالشَّمْسُ وَالقَمَرُ ۚ لَا تَسْجُدُوا لِلشَّمْسِ وَلَا لِلْقَمَرِ وَاسْجُدُوا لِلهِ الَّذِي خَلَقَهُنَّ إِنْ كُنْتُمْ إِيَّاهُ تَعْبُدُونَ﴾

Wa-d-dalīlu qawluhu taʿālā: ﴿Wa min āyātihi-l-laylu wa-n-nahāru wa-sh-shamsu wa-l-qamar, lā tasjudū li-sh-shamsi wa lā li-l-qamari wa-sjudū li-llāhi-lladhī khalaqahunna in kuntum iyyāhu taʿbudūn﴾

The proof is His saying, the Most High: {And from His signs are the night and the day, and the sun and the moon. Do not prostrate to the sun, nor to the moon, but prostrate to Allāh who created them — if you truly worship Him.} [41:37]

وَقَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿إِنَّ رَبَّكُمُ اللهُ الَّذِي خَلَقَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالأَرْضَ فِي سِتَّةِ أَيَّامٍ ثُمَّ اسْتَوَى عَلَى العَرْشِ ... أَلَا لَهُ الخَلْقُ وَالأَمْرُ ۗ تَبَارَكَ اللهُ رَبُّ العَالَمِينَ﴾

Wa qawluhu taʿālā: ﴿Inna rabbakumu-llāhu-lladhī khalaqa-s-samāwāti wa-l-arḍa fī sittati ayyāmin thumma-stawā ʿala-l-ʿarsh… alā lahu-l-khalqu wa-l-amr, tabāraka-llāhu rabbu-l-ʿālamīn﴾

And His saying, the Most High: {Indeed your Lord is Allāh, who created the heavens and the earth in six days, then ascended over the Throne… Unquestionably, His is the creation and the command. Blessed is Allāh, Lord of all creation.} [7:54]

Unit 2 · Lesson 8 — The Lord Is the One Worshipped
المَتْنُ — اُنْظُرْ وَاقْرَأْ وَتَدَبَّرْ

Workbook pp.11–13 — because Allāh alone is the Creator, He alone deserves worship; then the many types of worship are introduced.

وَالرَّبُّ هُوَ المَعْبُودُ. وَالدَّلِيلُ قَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿يَا أَيُّهَا النَّاسُ اعْبُدُوا رَبَّكُمُ الَّذِي خَلَقَكُمْ وَالَّذِينَ مِنْ قَبْلِكُمْ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَتَّقُونَ • الَّذِي جَعَلَ لَكُمُ الأَرْضَ فِرَاشًا وَالسَّمَاءَ بِنَاءً وَأَنْزَلَ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ مَاءً فَأَخْرَجَ بِهِ مِنَ الثَّمَرَاتِ رِزْقًا لَكُمْ فَلَا تَجْعَلُوا لِلهِ أَنْدَادًا وَأَنْتُمْ تَعْلَمُونَ﴾

Wa-r-rabbu huwa-l-maʿbūd. Wa-d-dalīlu qawluhu taʿālā: ﴿Yā ayyuha-n-nāsu-ʿbudū rabbakumu-lladhī khalaqakum wa-lladhīna min qablikum laʿallakum tattaqūn • alladhī jaʿala lakumu-l-arḍa firāshan wa-s-samāʾa bināʾan wa anzala mina-s-samāʾi māʾan fa-akhraja bihi mina-th-thamarāti rizqan lakum, fa-lā tajʿalū li-llāhi andādan wa antum taʿlamūn﴾

The Lord is the One who is worshipped. The proof is His saying, the Most High: {O mankind, worship your Lord who created you and those before you, that you may attain taqwā — Who made the earth a resting place for you and the sky a canopy, and sent down water from the sky, bringing forth fruits as provision for you. So do not set up rivals to Allāh while you know.} [2:21–22]

قَالَ ابْنُ كَثِيرٍ رَحِمَهُ اللهُ: «الخَالِقُ لِهَذِهِ الأَشْيَاءِ هُوَ المُسْتَحِقُّ لِلْعِبَادَةِ».

Qāla-bnu Kathīrin raḥimahu-llāh: «Al-khāliqu li-hādhihi-l-ashyāʾi huwa-l-mustaḥiqqu li-l-ʿibādah».

Ibn Kathīr (may Allāh have mercy upon him) said: "The One who created these things is the One who deserves to be worshipped."

🌱 Word root
أَنْدَاد · andād
Plural of nidd, from ن-د-د — a rival, an equal, a competitor. To "set up andād to Allāh" is to treat anything as His equal in love, fear, or worship. The verse forbids this "while you know" He alone created you.
أَنْوَاعُ العِبَادَةِ — The Types of Worship

وَأَنْوَاعُ العِبَادَةِ الَّتِي أَمَرَ اللهُ بِهَا مِثْلُ: الإِسْلَامِ وَالإِيمَانِ وَالإِحْسَانِ، وَمِنْهُ: الدُّعَاءُ وَالخَوْفُ وَالرَّجَاءُ وَالتَّوَكُّلُ وَالرَّغْبَةُ وَالرَّهْبَةُ وَالخُشُوعُ وَالخَشْيَةُ وَالإِنَابَةُ وَالاسْتِعَانَةُ وَالاسْتِعَاذَةُ وَالاسْتِغَاثَةُ وَالذَّبْحُ وَالنَّذْرُ وَغَيْرُ ذَلِكَ مِنْ أَنْوَاعِ العِبَادَةِ الَّتِي أَمَرَ اللهُ بِهَا، كُلُّهَا لِلهِ تَعَالَى.

Wa anwāʿu-l-ʿibādati-llatī amara-llāhu bihā mithlu: al-Islāmi wa-l-Īmāni wa-l-Iḥsān, wa minhu: ad-duʿāʾu wa-l-khawfu wa-r-rajāʾu wa-t-tawakkulu wa-r-raghbatu wa-r-rahbatu wa-l-khushūʿu wa-l-khashyatu wa-l-ināba­tu wa-l-istiʿānatu wa-l-istiʿādhatu wa-l-istighāthatu wa-dh-dhabḥu wa-n-nadhru wa ghayru dhālik… kulluhā li-llāhi taʿālā.

The types of worship Allāh commanded include Islām, Īmān, and Iḥsān; and among them: supplication, fear, hope, reliance, desire, dread, humility, reverent awe, turning in obedience, seeking help, seeking refuge, seeking rescue, sacrifice, vows, and other types Allāh commanded — all of them are for Allāh alone.

⚠️ The dividing line
This list is the heart of the First Principle. Each item is an act of worship. The rule that follows is absolute: directing any one of them to other than Allāh is shirk.

فَمَنْ صَرَفَ مِنْهَا شَيْئًا لِغَيْرِ اللهِ فَهُوَ مُشْرِكٌ كَافِرٌ. وَالدَّلِيلُ قَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَأَنَّ المَسَاجِدَ لِلهِ فَلَا تَدْعُوا مَعَ اللهِ أَحَدًا﴾ وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَمَنْ يَدْعُ مَعَ اللهِ إِلَهًا آخَرَ لَا بُرْهَانَ لَهُ بِهِ فَإِنَّمَا حِسَابُهُ عِنْدَ رَبِّهِ ۚ إِنَّهُ لَا يُفْلِحُ الكَافِرُونَ﴾

Fa-man ṣarafa minhā shayʾan li-ghayri-llāhi fa-huwa mushrikun kāfir. Wa-d-dalīlu qawluhu taʿālā: ﴿Wa anna-l-masājida li-llāhi fa-lā tadʿū maʿa-llāhi aḥadā﴾ wa qawluh: ﴿Wa man yadʿu maʿa-llāhi ilāhan ākhara lā burhāna lahu bihi fa-innamā ḥisābuhu ʿinda rabbih, innahu lā yufliḥu-l-kāfirūn﴾

Whoever directs any of it to other than Allāh is a Mushrik, a Kāfir. The proof is His saying: {And the places of worship are for Allāh, so do not invoke anyone along with Allāh} [72:18]. And: {Whoever invokes another deity alongside Allāh, for which he has no proof, his reckoning is only with his Lord. Indeed, the disbelievers will not succeed.} [23:117]

الأَدِلَّةُ — Each Type with Its Proof

Workbook pp.13–14 — the author gives a Qurʾānic (or Prophetic) proof that each act is worship owed to Allāh alone.

💡 How to read this lesson
For each act, ask: "Is this directed to Allāh's heart-and-deed?" If yes, it is worship — and worship belongs to Allāh alone. The author proves this one act at a time, so that no one can claim "this kind of devotion to other than Allāh is harmless."

الدُّعَاءُ
Ad-duʿāʾ — Supplication
The Prophet ﷺ said: «الدُّعَاءُ مُخُّ العِبَادَةِ» — "Supplication is the very core of worship."

﴿وَقَالَ رَبُّكُمُ ادْعُونِي أَسْتَجِبْ لَكُمْ ۚ إِنَّ الَّذِينَ يَسْتَكْبِرُونَ عَنْ عِبَادَتِي سَيَدْخُلُونَ جَهَنَّمَ دَاخِرِينَ﴾

﴿Wa qāla rabbukumu-dʿūnī astajib lakum, inna-lladhīna yastakbirūna ʿan ʿibādatī sayadkhulūna jahannama dākhirīn﴾

{Your Lord said: "Invoke Me, I will respond to you. Indeed, those who are too proud to worship Me will enter Hell in humiliation."} [40:60]

٢
الخَوْفُ
Al-khawf — Fear
Reverential fear of Allāh that drives one to obey Him.

﴿فَلَا تَخَافُوهُمْ وَخَافُونِ إِنْ كُنْتُمْ مُؤْمِنِينَ﴾

﴿Fa-lā takhāfūhum wa khāfūni in kuntum muʾminīn﴾

{So do not fear them, but fear Me, if you are truly believers.} [3:175]

٣
الرَّجَاءُ
Ar-rajāʾ — Hope
Hoping in Allāh's mercy and reward.

﴿فَمَنْ كَانَ يَرْجُو لِقَاءَ رَبِّهِ فَلْيَعْمَلْ عَمَلًا صَالِحًا وَلَا يُشْرِكْ بِعِبَادَةِ رَبِّهِ أَحَدًا﴾

﴿Fa-man kāna yarjū liqāʾa rabbihi fa-l-yaʿmal ʿamalan ṣāliḥan wa lā yushrik bi-ʿibādati rabbihi aḥadā﴾

{Whoever hopes for the meeting with his Lord, let him do righteous deeds and associate none in the worship of his Lord.} [18:110]

٤
التَّوَكُّلُ
At-tawakkul — Reliance
Trusting and depending upon Allāh alone.

﴿وَعَلَى اللهِ فَتَوَكَّلُوا إِنْ كُنْتُمْ مُؤْمِنِينَ﴾ ﴿وَمَنْ يَتَوَكَّلْ عَلَى اللهِ فَهُوَ حَسْبُهُ﴾

﴿Wa ʿala-llāhi fa-tawakkalū in kuntum muʾminīn﴾ ﴿wa man yatawakkal ʿala-llāhi fa-huwa ḥasbuh﴾

{And upon Allāh rely, if you are believers} [5:23]. {And whoever relies upon Allāh — He is sufficient for him} [65:3].

٥
الرَّغْبَةُ وَالرَّهْبَةُ وَالخُشُوعُ
Ar-raghbah, ar-rahbah, al-khushūʿ — Desire, dread & humility
Longing for His reward, dreading His punishment, and humbling oneself before Him.

﴿إِنَّهُمْ كَانُوا يُسَارِعُونَ فِي الخَيْرَاتِ وَيَدْعُونَنَا رَغَبًا وَرَهَبًا ۖ وَكَانُوا لَنَا خَاشِعِينَ﴾

﴿Innahum kānū yusāriʿūna fi-l-khayrāti wa yadʿūnanā raghaban wa rahaban, wa kānū lanā khāshiʿīn﴾

{They used to hasten to good deeds and call upon Us in hope and dread, and they were humble before Us.} [21:90]

٦
الخَشْيَةُ
Al-khashyah — Reverent awe
Fear coupled with knowledge of Allāh's greatness.

﴿فَلَا تَخْشَوْهُمْ وَاخْشَوْنِي﴾

﴿Fa-lā takhshawhum wa-khshawnī﴾

{So do not fear them, but stand in awe of Me.} [2:150]

٧
الإِنَابَةُ
Al-inābah — Turning in obedience
Returning to Allāh in repentance and submission.

﴿وَأَنِيبُوا إِلَى رَبِّكُمْ وَأَسْلِمُوا لَهُ﴾

﴿Wa anībū ilā rabbikum wa aslimū lah﴾

{And turn in repentance to your Lord and submit to Him.} [39:54]

🌱 Word root
التَّوَكُّل · at-tawakkul
From و-ك-ل — to entrust, to appoint as agent. Tawakkul is entrusting your affairs to Allāh while still taking the means. The same root gives wakīl (a trustee/agent) — and Allāh is the best Wakīl.

الأَدِلَّةُ — Each Type with Its Proof

Workbook pp.15–16 — the remaining types of worship that complete the First Principle.

٨
الاسْتِعَانَةُ
Al-istiʿānah — Seeking help
Seeking aid and support from Allāh.

﴿إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ﴾ وَفِي الحَدِيثِ: «وَإِذَا اسْتَعَنْتَ فَاسْتَعِنْ بِاللهِ».

﴿Iyyāka naʿbudu wa iyyāka nastaʿīn﴾ — wa fi-l-ḥadīth: «wa idha-staʿanta fa-staʿin bi-llāh».

{You alone we worship, and from You alone we seek help} [1:5]. And in the ḥadīth: "When you seek help, seek the help of Allāh."

٩
الاسْتِعَاذَةُ
Al-istiʿādhah — Seeking refuge
Seeking protection with Allāh from every harm.

﴿قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ الفَلَقِ﴾ ﴿قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ النَّاسِ﴾

﴿Qul aʿūdhu bi-rabbi-l-falaq﴾ ﴿qul aʿūdhu bi-rabbi-n-nās﴾

{Say: I seek refuge with the Lord of the daybreak} [113:1]. {Say: I seek refuge with the Lord of mankind} [114:1].

١٠
الاسْتِغَاثَةُ
Al-istighāthah — Seeking rescue
Seeking rescue from Allāh in distress and hardship.

﴿إِذْ تَسْتَغِيثُونَ رَبَّكُمْ فَاسْتَجَابَ لَكُمْ﴾

﴿Idh tastaghīthūna rabbakum fa-stajāba lakum﴾

{When you sought rescue from your Lord and He responded to you.} [8:9]

⚠️ A careful distinction
Asking a living, present person for ordinary help they can give ("pass me the water") is permitted and is not the worship meant here. The istiʿānah / istighāthah that is worship — and so belongs to Allāh alone — is seeking help in matters only Allāh controls (rescue from hidden harm, forgiveness, sustenance, guidance).
١١
الذَّبْحُ
Adh-dhabḥ — Sacrifice
Slaughtering an animal as an act of drawing near.

﴿قُلْ إِنَّ صَلَاتِي وَنُسُكِي وَمَحْيَايَ وَمَمَاتِي لِلهِ رَبِّ العَالَمِينَ • لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ وَبِذَلِكَ أُمِرْتُ وَأَنَا أَوَّلُ المُسْلِمِينَ﴾ وَمِنَ السُّنَّةِ: «لَعَنَ اللهُ مَنْ ذَبَحَ لِغَيْرِ اللهِ».

﴿Qul inna ṣalātī wa nusukī wa maḥyāya wa mamātī li-llāhi rabbi-l-ʿālamīn • lā sharīka lah, wa bi-dhālika umirtu wa anā awwalu-l-muslimīn﴾ wa mina-s-sunnah: «laʿana-llāhu man dhabaḥa li-ghayri-llāh».

{Say: Indeed my prayer, my sacrifice, my living, and my dying are for Allāh, Lord of all creation. He has no partner. With this I was commanded, and I am the first of the Muslims.} [6:162]. From the Sunnah: "Allāh has cursed the one who slaughters for other than Allāh."

النَّذْرُ
An-nadhr — Vows
Obliging oneself, for Allāh, to an act of worship.

﴿يُوفُونَ بِالنَّذْرِ وَيَخَافُونَ يَوْمًا كَانَ شَرُّهُ مُسْتَطِيرًا﴾

﴿Yūfūna bi-n-nadhri wa yakhāfūna yawman kāna sharruhu mustaṭīrā﴾

{They fulfil their vows and fear a day whose evil is widespread.} [76:7]

⭐ The First Principle, complete
Every act surveyed — from duʿāʾ to nadhr — is worship, and worship is owed to Allāh alone. That is the whole of the First Principle: to know your Lord is to single Him out in every act of devotion. The Second Principle (your religion) comes next.
🌱 Word root
الذَّبْح · adh-dhabḥ
From ذ-ب-ح — to slaughter. A sacrifice (dhabḥ) offered to draw near to anyone but Allāh is shirk — hence the ḥadīth's curse. The related nusuk in the verse means a ritual offering.

Unit 3 · Lesson 11 — Islām & the Testimony
المَتْنُ — اُنْظُرْ وَاقْرَأْ وَتَدَبَّرْ

Workbook pp.17–19 — the Second Principle, its definition, the three levels, and the first pillar of Islām.

💡 The Second Principle
After knowing your Lord, you must know your religion with proof. The author defines Islām, then divides the religion into three ascending levels — Islām, Īmān, Iḥsān — each treated in turn over this unit.

الأَصْلُ الثَّانِي: مَعْرِفَةُ دِينِ الإِسْلَامِ بِالأَدِلَّةِ. وَهُوَ: الاسْتِسْلَامُ لِلهِ بِالتَّوْحِيدِ، وَالانْقِيَادُ لَهُ بِالطَّاعَةِ، وَالبَرَاءَةُ مِنَ الشِّرْكِ وَأَهْلِهِ.

Al-aṣlu-th-thānī: maʿrifatu dīni-l-Islāmi bi-l-adillah. Wa huwa: al-istislāmu li-llāhi bi-t-tawḥīd, wa-l-inqiyādu lahu bi-ṭ-ṭāʿah, wa-l-barāʾatu mina-sh-shirki wa ahlih.

The Second Principle: knowledge of the religion of Islām with the proofs. It is: submission to Allāh with Tawḥīd, compliance with Him through obedience, and disassociation from shirk and its people.

وَهُوَ ثَلَاثُ مَرَاتِبَ: الإِسْلَامُ وَالإِيمَانُ وَالإِحْسَانُ. وَكُلُّ مَرْتَبَةٍ لَهَا أَرْكَانٌ. المَرْتَبَةُ الأُولَى: الإِسْلَامُ. فَأَرْكَانُ الإِسْلَامِ خَمْسَةٌ: شَهَادَةُ أَنْ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللهُ وَأَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا رَسُولُ اللهِ، وَإِقَامُ الصَّلَاةِ، وَإِيتَاءُ الزَّكَاةِ، وَصَوْمُ رَمَضَانَ، وَحَجُّ بَيْتِ اللهِ الحَرَامِ.

Wa huwa thalāthu marātib: al-Islāmu wa-l-Īmānu wa-l-Iḥsān. Wa kullu martabatin lahā arkān. Al-martabatu-l-ūlā: al-Islām. Fa-arkānu-l-Islāmi khamsah: shahādatu an lā ilāha illa-llāhu wa anna Muḥammadan rasūlu-llāh, wa iqāmu-ṣ-ṣalāh, wa ītāʾu-z-zakāh, wa ṣawmu Ramaḍān, wa ḥajju bayti-llāhi-l-ḥarām.

It is of three levels: Islām, Īmān, and Iḥsān. Each level has pillars. The first level is Islām, and its pillars are five: the testimony that none has the right to be worshipped but Allāh and that Muḥammad is the Messenger of Allāh; establishing the Ṣalāh; giving the Zakāh; fasting Ramaḍān; and Ḥajj to the Sacred House of Allāh.

فَدَلِيلُ الشَّهَادَةِ قَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿شَهِدَ اللهُ أَنَّهُ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ وَالمَلَائِكَةُ وَأُولُو العِلْمِ قَائِمًا بِالقِسْطِ ۚ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ العَزِيزُ الحَكِيمُ﴾

Fa-dalīlu-sh-shahādati qawluhu taʿālā: ﴿Shahida-llāhu annahu lā ilāha illā huwa wa-l-malāʾikatu wa ulu-l-ʿilmi qāʾiman bi-l-qisṭ, lā ilāha illā huwa-l-ʿazīzu-l-ḥakīm﴾

The proof for the testimony is His saying, the Most High: {Allāh bears witness that none has the right to be worshipped but Him, and so do the angels and the people of knowledge — maintaining justice. None has the right to be worshipped but Him, the Almighty, the All-Wise.} [3:18]

وَمَعْنَاهَا: لَا مَعْبُودَ بِحَقٍّ إِلَّا اللهُ. «لَا إِلَهَ» نَافِيًا جَمِيعَ مَا يُعْبَدُ مِنْ دُونِ اللهِ، «إِلَّا اللهُ» مُثْبِتًا العِبَادَةَ لِلهِ وَحْدَهُ لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ فِي عِبَادَتِهِ كَمَا أَنَّهُ لَيْسَ لَهُ شَرِيكٌ فِي مُلْكِهِ.

Wa maʿnāhā: lā maʿbūda bi-ḥaqqin illa-llāh. «Lā ilāha» nāfiyan jamīʿa mā yuʿbadu min dūni-llāh, «illa-llāhu» muthbitan al-ʿibādata li-llāhi waḥdahu lā sharīka lahu fī ʿibādatihi kamā annahu laysa lahu sharīkun fī mulkih.

Its meaning: none has the right to be worshipped except Allāh. "Lā ilāha" — negating everything worshipped besides Allāh; "illa-llāh" — affirming worship for Allāh alone, who has no partner in His worship just as He has no partner in His dominion.

🌱 Two halves of the shahādah
نَفْيٌ وَإِثْبَاتٌ · nafy & ithbāt
Lā ilāha = negation (nafy) — sweeping away every false object of worship. illā-llāh = affirmation (ithbāt) — fixing worship to Allāh alone. The whole creed turns on these two movements: empty the heart of all rivals, then fill it with Allāh.

وَتَفْسِيرُهَا الَّذِي يُوَضِّحُهَا قَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَإِذْ قَالَ إِبْرَاهِيمُ لِأَبِيهِ وَقَوْمِهِ إِنَّنِي بَرَاءٌ مِمَّا تَعْبُدُونَ • إِلَّا الَّذِي فَطَرَنِي فَإِنَّهُ سَيَهْدِينِ • وَجَعَلَهَا كَلِمَةً بَاقِيَةً فِي عَقِبِهِ لَعَلَّهُمْ يَرْجِعُونَ﴾ وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿قُلْ يَا أَهْلَ الكِتَابِ تَعَالَوْا إِلَى كَلِمَةٍ سَوَاءٍ بَيْنَنَا وَبَيْنَكُمْ أَلَّا نَعْبُدَ إِلَّا اللهَ وَلَا نُشْرِكَ بِهِ شَيْئًا...﴾

Wa tafsīruha-lladhī yuwaḍḍiḥuhā qawluhu taʿālā: ﴿Wa idh qāla Ibrāhīmu li-abīhi wa qawmihi innanī barāʾun mimmā taʿbudūn • illa-lladhī faṭaranī fa-innahu sa-yahdīn • wa jaʿalahā kalimatan bāqiyatan fī ʿaqibihi laʿallahum yarjiʿūn﴾ wa qawluh: ﴿Qul yā ahla-l-kitābi taʿālaw ilā kalimatin sawāʾin baynanā wa baynakum allā naʿbuda illa-llāha wa lā nushrika bihi shayʾā…﴾

Its clarifying explanation is His saying: {And when Ibrāhīm said to his father and his people: "I am free of what you worship — except the One who created me; for He will guide me." And he made it a word enduring among his descendants, that they might return.} [43:26–28]. And: {Say: O People of the Book, come to a word common between us and you — that we worship none but Allāh and associate nothing with Him…} [3:64]

Workbook pp.19–20 — the second half of the testimony, and the proofs for Ṣalāh, Zakāh, fasting, and Ḥajj.

وَدَلِيلُ شَهَادَةِ أَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا رَسُولُ اللهِ قَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿لَقَدْ جَاءَكُمْ رَسُولٌ مِنْ أَنْفُسِكُمْ عَزِيزٌ عَلَيْهِ مَا عَنِتُّمْ حَرِيصٌ عَلَيْكُمْ بِالمُؤْمِنِينَ رَؤُوفٌ رَحِيمٌ﴾

Wa dalīlu shahādati anna Muḥammadan rasūlu-llāhi qawluhu taʿālā: ﴿Laqad jāʾakum rasūlun min anfusikum ʿazīzun ʿalayhi mā ʿanittum ḥarīṣun ʿalaykum bi-l-muʾminīna raʾūfun raḥīm﴾

The proof for the testimony that Muḥammad is the Messenger of Allāh is His saying: {There has certainly come to you a Messenger from among yourselves. Grievous to him is your suffering; he is concerned over you, and to the believers kind and merciful.} [9:128]

وَمَعْنَى شَهَادَةِ أَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا رَسُولُ اللهِ: طَاعَتُهُ فِيمَا أَمَرَ، وَتَصْدِيقُهُ فِيمَا أَخْبَرَ، وَاجْتِنَابُ مَا نَهَى عَنْهُ وَزَجَرَ، وَأَلَّا يُعْبَدَ اللهُ إِلَّا بِمَا شَرَعَ.

Wa maʿnā shahādati anna Muḥammadan rasūlu-llāh: ṭāʿatuhu fīmā amar, wa taṣdīquhu fīmā akhbar, wa-jtinābu mā nahā ʿanhu wa zajar, wa allā yuʿbada-llāhu illā bimā sharaʿ.

The meaning of the testimony that Muḥammad is the Messenger of Allāh: to obey him in what he commanded, to believe him in what he reported, to avoid what he forbade and prohibited, and that Allāh is not worshipped except by what he legislated.

⭐ Four duties in one testimony
Saying "Muḥammad is the Messenger of Allāh" is a pledge of four things: obey his commands, believe his reports, shun his prohibitions, and worship Allāh only as he taught. The last point is decisive — it rules out invented acts of worship.

وَدَلِيلُ الصَّلَاةِ وَالزَّكَاةِ وَتَفْسِيرِ التَّوْحِيدِ قَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَمَا أُمِرُوا إِلَّا لِيَعْبُدُوا اللهَ مُخْلِصِينَ لَهُ الدِّينَ حُنَفَاءَ وَيُقِيمُوا الصَّلَاةَ وَيُؤْتُوا الزَّكَاةَ ۚ وَذَلِكَ دِينُ القَيِّمَةِ﴾

Wa dalīlu-ṣ-ṣalāti wa-z-zakāti wa tafsīri-t-tawḥīdi qawluhu taʿālā: ﴿Wa mā umirū illā li-yaʿbudu-llāha mukhliṣīna lahu-d-dīna ḥunafāʾa wa yuqīmu-ṣ-ṣalāta wa yuʾtu-z-zakāh, wa dhālika dīnu-l-qayyimah﴾

The proof for Ṣalāh, Zakāh, and the explanation of Tawḥīd is His saying: {They were commanded only to worship Allāh, devoting the religion purely to Him as people of pure faith, and to establish the Ṣalāh and give the Zakāh — that is the upright religion.} [98:5]

وَدَلِيلُ الصِّيَامِ قَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا كُتِبَ عَلَيْكُمُ الصِّيَامُ كَمَا كُتِبَ عَلَى الَّذِينَ مِنْ قَبْلِكُمْ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَتَّقُونَ﴾

Wa dalīlu-ṣ-ṣiyāmi qawluhu taʿālā: ﴿Yā ayyuha-lladhīna āmanū kutiba ʿalaykumu-ṣ-ṣiyāmu kamā kutiba ʿala-lladhīna min qablikum laʿallakum tattaqūn﴾

The proof for fasting is His saying: {O you who believe, fasting is prescribed for you as it was prescribed for those before you, that you may attain taqwā.} [2:183]

وَدَلِيلُ الحَجِّ قَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَلِلَّهِ عَلَى النَّاسِ حِجُّ البَيْتِ مَنِ اسْتَطَاعَ إِلَيْهِ سَبِيلًا ۚ وَمَنْ كَفَرَ فَإِنَّ اللهَ غَنِيٌّ عَنِ العَالَمِينَ﴾

Wa dalīlu-l-ḥajji qawluhu taʿālā: ﴿Wa li-llāhi ʿala-n-nāsi ḥijju-l-bayti mani-staṭāʿa ilayhi sabīlā, wa man kafara fa-inna-llāha ghaniyyun ʿani-l-ʿālamīn﴾

The proof for Ḥajj is His saying: {And Ḥajj to the House is a duty owed to Allāh by people who are able to find a way to it. And whoever disbelieves, then Allāh is free of need of all creation.} [3:97]

📖 The workbook lists Ḥajj among the five pillars; its proof (3:97) is the well-known Qurʾānic citation completing the set.

Unit 3 · Lesson 13 — The Second Level: Īmān
المَتْنُ — اُنْظُرْ وَاقْرَأْ وَتَدَبَّرْ

Workbook pp.21–22 — the second level, its many branches, and its six pillars.

المَرْتَبَةُ الثَّانِيَةُ: الإِيمَانُ. وَهُوَ بِضْعٌ وَسَبْعُونَ شُعْبَةً، أَعْلَاهَا قَوْلُ «لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللهُ»، وَأَدْنَاهَا إِمَاطَةُ الأَذَى عَنِ الطَّرِيقِ، وَالحَيَاءُ شُعْبَةٌ مِنَ الإِيمَانِ.

Al-martabatu-th-thāniyah: al-Īmān. Wa huwa biḍʿun wa sabʿūna shuʿbah, aʿlāhā qawlu «lā ilāha illa-llāh», wa adnāhā imāṭatu-l-adhā ʿani-ṭ-ṭarīq, wa-l-ḥayāʾu shuʿbatun mina-l-īmān.

The second level is Īmān. It is some seventy-odd branches; the highest is saying "Lā ilāha illā-llāh," the lowest is removing harm from the road, and modesty (ḥayāʾ) is a branch of faith.

🌱 Word root
الإِيمَان · al-īmān
From أ-م-ن — security, safety, trust. Īmān is to affirm and entrust the heart with full security. The same root gives amān (safety) and amānah (a trust). Faith spans from the greatest word (Tawḥīd) down to the smallest deed (clearing the path).

وَأَرْكَانُهُ سِتَّةٌ: أَنْ تُؤْمِنَ بِاللهِ، وَمَلَائِكَتِهِ، وَكُتُبِهِ، وَرُسُلِهِ، وَاليَوْمِ الآخِرِ، وَبِالقَدَرِ خَيْرِهِ وَشَرِّهِ.

Wa arkānuhu sittah: an tuʾmina bi-llāhi, wa malāʾikatih, wa kutubih, wa rusulih, wa-l-yawmi-l-ākhir, wa bi-l-qadari khayrihi wa sharrih.

Its pillars are six: to believe in Allāh, His angels, His books, His messengers, the Last Day, and in the Decree (qadar) — its good and its bad.

⭐ Five vs six

Unit 3 · Lesson 14 — The Third Level: Iḥsān
المَتْنُ — اُنْظُرْ وَاقْرَأْ وَتَدَبَّرْ

Workbook p.22 — the highest level: worshipping Allāh as though you see Him.

💡 What is Iḥsān?
Iḥsān is the excellence of worship: doing it with such presence of heart that you behave as if you can see Allāh — and even when you cannot, you never forget that He sees you. It is a single pillar, but it perfects the other two levels.

المَرْتَبَةُ الثَّالِثَةُ: الإِحْسَانُ، وَلَهُ رُكْنٌ وَاحِدٌ. كَمَا فِي الحَدِيثِ: «أَنْ تَعْبُدَ اللهَ كَأَنَّكَ تَرَاهُ، فَإِنْ لَمْ تَكُنْ تَرَاهُ فَإِنَّهُ يَرَاكَ».

Al-martabatu-th-thālithah: al-Iḥsān, wa lahu ruknun wāḥid. Kamā fi-l-ḥadīth: «an taʿbuda-llāha kaʾannaka tarāh, fa-in lam takun tarāhu fa-innahu yarāk».

The third level is Iḥsān, and it has one pillar. As in the ḥadīth: "It is to worship Allāh as if you see Him; and if you do not see Him, then (know that) He sees you."

وَالدَّلِيلُ قَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿إِنَّ اللهَ مَعَ الَّذِينَ اتَّقَوْا وَالَّذِينَ هُمْ مُحْسِنُونَ﴾

Wa-d-dalīlu qawluhu taʿālā: ﴿Inna-llāha maʿa-lladhīna-ttaqaw wa-lladhīna hum muḥsinūn﴾

Wa qawluhu taʿālā: ﴿Wa tawakkal ʿala-l-ʿazīzi-r-raḥīm • alladhī yarāka ḥīna taqūm • wa taqallubaka fi-s-sājidīn • innahu huwa-s-samīʿu-l-ʿalīm﴾

وَقَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَمَا تَكُونُ فِي شَأْنٍ وَمَا تَتْلُو مِنْهُ مِنْ قُرْآنٍ وَلَا تَعْمَلُونَ مِنْ عَمَلٍ إِلَّا كُنَّا عَلَيْكُمْ شُهُودًا إِذْ تُفِيضُونَ فِيهِ﴾

Wa qawluhu taʿālā: ﴿Wa mā takūnu fī shaʾnin wa mā tatlū minhu min qurʾānin wa lā taʿmalūna min ʿamalin illā kunnā ʿalaykum shuhūdan idh tufīḍūna fīh﴾

And His saying: {You are not engaged in any matter, nor do you recite any portion of the Qurʾān, nor do you perform any deed, except that We are Witness over you when you are engaged in it.} [10:61]

🌱 Word root
الإِحْسَان · al-iḥsān
From ح-س-ن — to be good, beautiful, excellent. Iḥsān is to do a thing beautifully, to its utmost. A muḥsin is one who worships Allāh with that beauty and awareness. The same root gives ḥusn (beauty) and aḥsan (better, best).

Unit 3 · Lesson 15 — The Ḥadīth of Jibrīl (the Proof from the Sunnah)
الدَّلِيلُ مِنَ السُّنَّةِ — اُنْظُرْ وَاقْرَأْ وَتَدَبَّرْ

Workbook pp.23–25 — the famous ḥadīth of Jibrīl, narrated by ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb, gathering all three levels in one conversation.

⭐ One ḥadīth, the whole religion
This single ḥadīth is the Sunnah-proof for the entire Second Principle: it defines Islām (5 pillars), Īmān (6 pillars), and Iḥsān (1 pillar) in turn — which is why the Prophet ﷺ ended by saying Jibrīl "came to teach you your religion."

عُمَرُ ﷺ : بَيْنَمَا نَحْنُ جُلُوسٌ عِنْدَ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ، إِذْ طَلَعَ عَلَيْنَا رَجُلٌ شَدِيدُ بَيَاضِ الثِّيَابِ، شَدِيدُ سَوَادِ الشَّعْرِ، لَا يُرَى عَلَيْهِ أَثَرُ السَّفَرِ، وَلَا يَعْرِفُهُ مِنَّا أَحَدٌ، فَجَلَسَ إِلَى النَّبِيِّ ﷺ فَأَسْنَدَ رُكْبَتَيْهِ إِلَى رُكْبَتَيْهِ وَوَضَعَ كَفَّيْهِ عَلَى فَخِذَيْهِ، وَقَالَ:

ʿUmar: Baynamā naḥnu julūsun ʿinda-n-nabiyyi ﷺ, idh ṭalaʿa ʿalaynā rajulun shadīdu bayāḍi-th-thiyāb, shadīdu sawādi-sh-shaʿr, lā yurā ʿalayhi atharu-s-safar, wa lā yaʿrifuhu minnā aḥad, fa-jalasa ila-n-nabiyyi ﷺ fa-asnada rukbatayhi ilā rukbatayhi wa waḍaʿa kaffayhi ʿalā fakhidhayhi, wa qāl:

ʿUmar said: While we were sitting with the Prophet ﷺ, a man appeared with extremely white clothes and extremely black hair. No sign of travel was on him, and none of us knew him. He sat before the Prophet ﷺ, resting his knees against his and placing his palms on his thighs, and said:

جِبْرِيلُ : يَا مُحَمَّدُ، أَخْبِرْنِي عَنِ الإِسْلَامِ.

Jibrīl: Yā Muḥammad, akhbirnī ʿani-l-Islām.

Jibrīl: O Muḥammad, tell me about Islām.

النَّبِيُّ ﷺ : الإِسْلَامُ أَنْ تَشْهَدَ أَنْ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللهُ وَأَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا رَسُولُ اللهِ، وَتُقِيمَ الصَّلَاةَ، وَتُؤْتِيَ الزَّكَاةَ، وَتَصُومَ رَمَضَانَ، وَتَحُجَّ البَيْتَ إِنِ اسْتَطَعْتَ إِلَيْهِ سَبِيلًا.

An-Nabī ﷺ: Al-Islāmu an tashhada an lā ilāha illa-llāhu wa anna Muḥammadan rasūlu-llāh, wa tuqīma-ṣ-ṣalāh, wa tuʾtiya-z-zakāh, wa taṣūma Ramaḍān, wa taḥujja-l-bayta ini-staṭaʿta ilayhi sabīlā.

The Prophet ﷺ: Islām is to testify that none has the right to be worshipped but Allāh and that Muḥammad is the Messenger of Allāh, to establish the Ṣalāh, to give the Zakāh, to fast Ramaḍān, and to make Ḥajj to the House if you are able.

جِبْرِيلُ : صَدَقْتَ. (فَعَجِبْنَا لَهُ يَسْأَلُهُ وَيُصَدِّقُهُ)

Jibrīl: Ṣadaqt. (We were amazed: he asks, then confirms the answer!)

Jibrīl: You have spoken truthfully. — And we were amazed that he would ask, then affirm the answer.

جِبْرِيلُ : أَخْبِرْنِي عَنِ الإِيمَانِ.

Jibrīl: Akhbirnī ʿani-l-īmān.

Jibrīl: Tell me about Īmān.

النَّبِيُّ ﷺ : أَنْ تُؤْمِنَ بِاللهِ وَمَلَائِكَتِهِ وَكُتُبِهِ وَرُسُلِهِ وَاليَوْمِ الآخِرِ، وَتُؤْمِنَ بِالقَدَرِ خَيْرِهِ وَشَرِّهِ.

An-Nabī ﷺ: An tuʾmina bi-llāhi wa malāʾikatihi wa kutubihi wa rusulihi wa-l-yawmi-l-ākhir, wa tuʾmina bi-l-qadari khayrihi wa sharrih.

The Prophet ﷺ: To believe in Allāh, His angels, His books, His messengers, the Last Day, and to believe in the Decree — its good and its bad.

جِبْرِيلُ : صَدَقْتَ. أَخْبِرْنِي عَنِ الإِحْسَانِ.

Jibrīl: Ṣadaqt. Akhbirnī ʿani-l-iḥsān.

Jibrīl: You have spoken truthfully. Tell me about Iḥsān.

النَّبِيُّ ﷺ : أَنْ تَعْبُدَ اللهَ كَأَنَّكَ تَرَاهُ، فَإِنْ لَمْ تَكُنْ تَرَاهُ فَإِنَّهُ يَرَاكَ.

An-Nabī ﷺ: An taʿbuda-llāha kaʾannaka tarāh, fa-in lam takun tarāhu fa-innahu yarāk.

The Prophet ﷺ: To worship Allāh as if you see Him; and if you do not see Him, then He sees you.

جِبْرِيلُ : أَخْبِرْنِي عَنِ السَّاعَةِ.

Jibrīl: Akhbirnī ʿani-s-sāʿah.

Jibrīl: Tell me about the Hour.

💡 The Third Principle
The final principle is knowing your Prophet ﷺ: his identity and lineage, the stages of his life and call, his message, and what he was sent with. To know him is to know whom you must follow.

الأَصْلُ الثَّالِثُ: مَعْرِفَةُ نَبِيِّكُمْ مُحَمَّدٍ ﷺ. وَهُوَ مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ عَبْدِ اللهِ بْنِ عَبْدِ المُطَّلِبِ بْنِ هَاشِمٍ، وَهَاشِمٌ مِنْ قُرَيْشٍ، وَقُرَيْشٌ مِنَ العَرَبِ، وَالعَرَبُ مِنْ ذُرِّيَّةِ إِسْمَاعِيلَ بْنِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ الخَلِيلِ عَلَيْهِ وَعَلَى نَبِيِّنَا أَفْضَلُ الصَّلَاةِ وَالسَّلَامِ.

Al-aṣlu-th-thālith: maʿrifatu nabiyyikum Muḥammad ﷺ. Wa huwa Muḥammadu-bnu ʿAbdi-llāhi-bni ʿAbdi-l-Muṭṭalibi-bni Hāshim, wa Hāshimun min Quraysh, wa Qurayshun mina-l-ʿArab, wa-l-ʿArabu min dhurriyyati Ismāʿīla-bni Ibrāhīma-l-Khalīl, ʿalayhi wa ʿalā nabiyyinā afḍalu-ṣ-ṣalāti wa-s-salām.

The Third Principle: knowledge of your Prophet Muḥammad ﷺ. He is Muḥammad, son of ʿAbdullāh, son of ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib, son of Hāshim. Hāshim is from Quraysh, Quraysh is from the Arabs, and the Arabs are from the offspring of Ismāʿīl, son of Ibrāhīm the Khalīl — upon him and upon our Prophet be the best of blessings and peace.

وَلَهُ مِنَ العُمُرِ ثَلَاثٌ وَسِتُّونَ سَنَةً، مِنْهَا أَرْبَعُونَ قَبْلَ النُّبُوَّةِ، وَثَلَاثٌ وَعِشْرُونَ نَبِيًّا رَسُولًا. نُبِّئَ بِـ﴿اقْرَأْ﴾ وَأُرْسِلَ بِـ﴿المُدَّثِّرِ﴾، وَبَلَدُهُ مَكَّةُ، وَهَاجَرَ إِلَى المَدِينَةِ.

Wa lahu mina-l-ʿumuri thalāthun wa sittūna sanah, minhā arbaʿūna qabla-n-nubuwwah, wa thalāthun wa ʿishrūna nabiyyan rasūlā. Nubbiʾa bi-﴿Iqraʾ﴾ wa ursila bi-﴿al-Muddaththir﴾, wa baladuhu Makkah, wa hājara ilā-l-Madīnah.

He lived sixty-three years: forty before prophethood, and twenty-three as a prophet and messenger. He was made a Prophet with {Iqraʾ} (Sūrah 96) and a Messenger with {al-Muddaththir} (Sūrah 74). His city was Makkah, and he migrated to Madīnah.

بَعَثَهُ اللهُ بِالنِّذَارَةِ عَنِ الشِّرْكِ وَالدَّعْوَةِ إِلَى التَّوْحِيدِ. وَالدَّلِيلُ قَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿يَا أَيُّهَا المُدَّثِّرُ • قُمْ فَأَنْذِرْ • وَرَبَّكَ فَكَبِّرْ • وَثِيَابَكَ فَطَهِّرْ • وَالرُّجْزَ فَاهْجُرْ • وَلَا تَمْنُنْ تَسْتَكْثِرُ • وَلِرَبِّكَ فَاصْبِرْ﴾

Baʿathahu-llāhu bi-n-nidhārati ʿani-sh-shirki wa-d-daʿwati ila-t-tawḥīd. Wa-d-dalīlu qawluhu taʿālā: ﴿Yā ayyuha-l-muddaththir • qum fa-andhir • wa rabbaka fa-kabbir • wa thiyābaka fa-ṭahhir • wa-r-rujza fa-hjur • wa lā tamnun tastakthir • wa li-rabbika fa-ṣbir﴾

Allāh sent him to warn against shirk and to call to Tawḥīd. The proof is His saying: {O you who are enwrapped! Arise and warn! Exalt your Lord! Purify your garments! Keep away from ar-Rujz (idols)! Give not, expecting more! And be patient for the sake of your Lord!} [74:1–7]

Unit 4 · Lesson 17 — Stages of the Daʿwah & the Hijrah
المَتْنُ — اُنْظُرْ وَاقْرَأْ وَتَدَبَّرْ

Workbook pp.27–28 — ten years of calling, the Miʿrāj, and the migration that remains until the Hour.

أَخَذَ عَلَى هَذَا عَشْرَ سِنِينَ يَدْعُو إِلَى التَّوْحِيدِ، وَبَعْدَ العَشْرِ عُرِجَ بِهِ إِلَى السَّمَاءِ وَفُرِضَتْ عَلَيْهِ الصَّلَوَاتُ الخَمْسُ، وَصَلَّى فِي مَكَّةَ ثَلَاثَ سِنِينَ، وَبَعْدَهَا أُمِرَ بِالهِجْرَةِ إِلَى المَدِينَةِ.

Akhadha ʿalā hādhā ʿashra sinīna yadʿū ila-t-tawḥīd, wa baʿda-l-ʿashri ʿurija bihi ila-s-samāʾi wa furiḍat ʿalayhi-ṣ-ṣalawātu-l-khams, wa ṣallā fī Makkata thalātha sinīn, wa baʿdahā umira bi-l-hijrati ila-l-Madīnah.

He spent ten years upon this, calling to Tawḥīd. After the ten years he was taken up to the heaven (the Miʿrāj), and the five prayers were made obligatory upon him. He prayed in Makkah for three years, and then was ordered to migrate to Madīnah.

وَالهِجْرَةُ: الانْتِقَالُ مِنْ بَلَدِ الشِّرْكِ إِلَى بَلَدِ الإِسْلَامِ، وَهِيَ بَاقِيَةٌ إِلَى أَنْ تَقُومَ السَّاعَةُ.

Wa-l-hijrah: al-intiqālu min baladi-sh-shirki ilā baladi-l-Islām, wa hiya bāqiyatun ilā an taqūma-s-sāʿah.

The Hijrah is: moving from a land of shirk to a land of Islām — and it remains in force until the Hour is established.

🌱 Word root
الهِجْرَة · al-hijrah
From ه-ج-ر — to abandon, leave, forsake. The Hijrah is leaving the land of disbelief for the sake of one's religion. The same root gives hajr (to abandon something) and muhājir (an emigrant for Allāh's cause).

وَالدَّلِيلُ قَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿إِنَّ الَّذِينَ تَوَفَّاهُمُ المَلَائِكَةُ ظَالِمِي أَنْفُسِهِمْ قَالُوا فِيمَ كُنْتُمْ قَالُوا كُنَّا مُسْتَضْعَفِينَ فِي الأَرْضِ قَالُوا أَلَمْ تَكُنْ أَرْضُ اللهِ وَاسِعَةً فَتُهَاجِرُوا فِيهَا ۚ فَأُولَئِكَ مَأْوَاهُمْ جَهَنَّمُ وَسَاءَتْ مَصِيرًا﴾

Wa-d-dalīlu qawluhu taʿālā: ﴿Inna-lladhīna tawaffāhumu-l-malāʾikatu ẓālimī anfusihim qālū fīma kuntum, qālū kunnā mustaḍʿafīna fi-l-arḍ, qālū alam takun arḍu-llāhi wāsiʿatan fa-tuhājirū fīhā, fa-ulāʾika maʾwāhum jahannamu wa sāʾat maṣīrā﴾

The proof is His saying: {Indeed, those whom the angels take (in death) while wronging themselves — they say: "In what condition were you?" They reply: "We were oppressed on earth." They say: "Was not the earth of Allāh spacious enough for you to migrate within it?" For those, their refuge is Hell — an evil destination.} [4:97]

وَقَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿يَا عِبَادِيَ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا إِنَّ أَرْضِي وَاسِعَةٌ فَإِيَّايَ فَاعْبُدُونِ﴾ قَالَ البَغَوِيُّ رَحِمَهُ اللهُ: «نَزَلَتْ هَذِهِ الآيَةُ فِي المُسْلِمِينَ الَّذِينَ بِمَكَّةَ لَمْ يُهَاجِرُوا، نَادَاهُمُ اللهُ بِاسْمِ الإِيمَانِ».

Wa qawluhu taʿālā: ﴿Yā ʿibādiya-lladhīna āmanū inna arḍī wāsiʿatun fa-iyyāya fa-ʿbudūn﴾. Qāla-l-Baghawiyyu raḥimahu-llāh: «Nazalat hādhihi-l-āyatu fi-l-muslimīna-lladhīna bi-Makkata lam yuhājirū, nādāhumu-llāhu bi-smi-l-īmān».

And His saying: {O My servants who believe! Indeed My earth is spacious, so worship Me (alone).} [29:56] Al-Baghawī said: "This verse was revealed concerning the Muslims in Makkah who had not yet migrated — and Allāh addressed them by the name of faith (as believers)."

وَالدَّلِيلُ مِنَ السُّنَّةِ: «لَا تَنْقَطِعُ الهِجْرَةُ حَتَّى تَنْقَطِعَ التَّوْبَةُ، وَلَا تَنْقَطِعُ التَّوْبَةُ حَتَّى تَطْلُعَ الشَّمْسُ مِنْ مَغْرِبِهَا».

Wa-d-dalīlu mina-s-sunnah: «Lā tanqaṭiʿu-l-hijratu ḥattā tanqaṭiʿa-t-tawbah, wa lā tanqaṭiʿu-t-tawbatu ḥattā taṭluʿa-sh-shamsu min maghribihā».

The proof from the Sunnah: "Hijrah shall not cease until repentance ceases; and repentance shall not cease until the sun rises from its place of setting (the west)."

⭐ Hijrah did not end
A common misunderstanding is that Hijrah belonged only to the Prophet's generation. The matn — and this ḥadīth — make clear it continues until the Hour: whenever a believer cannot practise the religion, migrating for its sake remains a live ruling.

الانْتِقَالُ مِنْ بَلَدِ الشِّرْكِ إِلَى بَلَدِ الإِسْلَامِ، وَهِيَ بَاقِيَةٌ إِلَى أَنْ تَقُومَ السَّاعَةُMoving from a land of shirk to a land of Islām; it remains until the Hour.
